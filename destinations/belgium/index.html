Where we work

Belgian cities we know inside out.

Nowhere in Belgium is more than a couple of hours away — one programme can comfortably combine two or three of these.

Capital · our base

Brussels

The Grand-Place, Art Nouveau, the EU quarter and serious conference capacity — plus the restaurant scene to end every programme well.

Medieval jewel

Bruges

Canals, cobbles and candlelit venues — the incentive favourite, at its best once the day crowds have gone home.

Authentic Flanders

Ghent

Bruges' livelier sibling: waterside guild houses, a castle in the city centre and a food scene locals actually use.

Port & fashion

Antwerp

Rubens, diamonds and design — striking venues from the old exchange to the port house, and arena-scale events.

University town

Leuven

A grand Gothic town hall, a centuries-old university and one of the world's great beer traditions on its doorstep.

South & outdoors

Liège & the Ardennes

Wallonia's biggest city and the forested hills beyond — outdoor team programmes, château dinners and winter atmosphere.

Belgium FAQ

Common questions about Belgian programmes.

More general questions are answered on our full FAQ page.

Which Scancoming office handles trips to Belgium?+

Belgium is home turf: our Benelux office is based in Brussels. Your programme is planned and booked by people who live where your group is going — and who are reachable out of hours.

Do you cover both Flanders and Wallonia?+

Yes. Our team works with suppliers in Dutch and French across both language regions — from Bruges and Antwerp to Liège and the Ardennes — while you keep one English-speaking contact.

Can you organise events near the EU quarter in Brussels?+

Yes. Meetings, dinners and delegate logistics around the European quarter are a regular part of our Brussels work, from small board-level dinners to multi-day association programmes.

Is Bruges better as a day trip or an overnight stay?+

Both work — it depends on your programme. Bruges makes an easy day excursion from Brussels, but an overnight stay lets your group see the city after the day visitors leave. We will recommend what fits your goal and budget.

Can a Belgian programme be combined with the Netherlands or Luxembourg?+

Very easily — this is exactly why one office covers all three countries. Brussels to Amsterdam is under two hours by train, Luxembourg around three by road, and one team handles the whole cross-border itinerary.

Planning something in Belgium?

Send the goal, the group and the dates to our Brussels team. We'll come back fast with a tailor-made plan.
