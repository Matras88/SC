About Scancoming

What is Scancoming?+

Scancoming is a destination management company (DMC) established in 1977. We arrange corporate meetings, incentives, tailor-made tours, city breaks and event tickets across the UK, Ireland and Benelux — with local offices in London, Dublin and Brussels. If the term DMC is new to you, our guide What is a DMC? explains it in plain English.

Which countries do you cover?+

Five: the United Kingdom, Ireland, the Netherlands, Belgium and Luxembourg. Each is served by the local office closest to it — London, Dublin or Brussels.

Who do you work with?+

Corporate clients planning their own meetings and incentives, event and incentive agencies who need a local ground partner, and tour operators who resell our itineraries. The service model is the same for all: one brief, one named regional contact.

How long have you been operating?+

Since 1977 — nearly five decades of doing one thing well: being the dependable local partner that international clients can hand the hard part to.

Enquiries & how we work

How do I send an enquiry?+

Email the office closest to your destination — uk@scancoming.com (UK), ireland@scancoming.com (Ireland) or benelux@scancoming.com (Netherlands, Belgium, Luxembourg) — or send a general brief to hello@scancoming.com and we will route it. Include the goal, the group size, the dates and the budget if you have one.

What happens after I enquire?+

Three steps. First, we clarify the brief with you — one conversation. Then our local team researches venues, negotiates rates and builds a tailor-made proposal. Once you approve it, we handle the bookings, the contracts and the on-site delivery.

Will I have one contact or be passed around?+

One named contact in the regional office owns your account from first brief to final delivery. That person is who you email, who you call, and who answers.

What if something goes wrong during the trip?+

Every region has an out-of-hours emergency line, published in your programme documents. Travel doesn't keep office hours, and neither do we when your group is on the ground.

Destinations & programmes

Which office handles which destination?+

London handles the UK; Dublin handles the island of Ireland; Brussels handles the Netherlands, Belgium and Luxembourg. For cross-border programmes the offices work as one team behind a single contact.

Can one programme cover several countries?+

Yes — it is one of the most useful things about our setup. London + Dublin, or Amsterdam + Brussels + Luxembourg, can be planned as one itinerary with one brief and one proposal.

What group sizes do you handle?+

From a board meeting of eight to conferences, incentives and touring groups of several hundred. Tell us the number honestly — including how it might change — and we plan around it.

How far in advance should I book?+

The earlier the better for high-demand periods — spring in the Netherlands, festival season in Edinburgh and Dublin, major sporting weekends anywhere. But a speedy response is part of our promise, and short-notice programmes are a regular part of our work. Ask; we will tell you honestly what is achievable.

Can you accommodate dietary requirements and accessibility needs?+

Yes — tell us when you brief us and we build it in: menus agreed with restaurants in advance, step-free venues and transport where needed, and pacing that suits the group.

Pricing & booking

How is a programme priced?+

Against your brief. There are no off-the-shelf packages: you receive one itemised, tailor-made proposal covering venues, accommodation, transport, guiding, meals and tickets. You see the costs before anything is committed.

What are the payment and cancellation terms?+

Terms vary with the programme, because they follow the conditions of the venues and suppliers involved — a theatre allocation behaves differently from a hotel block. Your proposal states the exact payment schedule and cancellation terms in writing before you confirm.

What if my group size changes after booking?+

Tell us as early as possible. Where supplier terms allow it we adjust the bookings; where they don't, we tell you the cost implications straight away rather than at the invoice.

Do you book international flights?+

Our work is on the ground: everything from the moment your group arrives to the moment it leaves. Most clients book their own flights; we plan the programme around your arrival and departure times, transfers included.

Question answered? Send the brief.

The goal, the group and the dates — that's all we need to come back fast with a tailor-made plan.
